Starting the car

The ignition switch lets the operator engage the engine as a precursor to starting the car. When the ignition is 'on' the radio and heater will run . The ignition must be in 'start' to start the engine.

The ignition switch is located in the central of the control panel and is operated by a key. The switch has two position: 'on' and 'start'.
  1. Insert the key into the ignition switch.
  2. Turn the key clockwise to the 'on' position.
    The ignition warning light displays.
    Important: Leaving the ignition switch in the 'on' position when the engine is not running will draw power from the battery.
  3. Note: The gear lever must be in the neutral position.
    Turn the key clockwise to the 'start' position.
  4. Press the starter switch quickly and firmly.
    The engine starts.
    Important: Continuing to hold the starter switch down if the engine fails to start draws a heavy current and may completely discharge the battery.
